‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to respond to an eviction notice?
In Minnesota, after being served with an eviction summons and complaint, you typically have 7 days to file an answer with the court. If you don't respond, you may receive a default judgment against you. Use Semptify's Answer Generator to create your response quickly.
Can I be evicted in winter in Minnesota?
Yes, evictions can proceed in winter. However, sheriffs will typically not execute a physical removal if temperatures are dangerously cold and there's no shelter available. This is discretionary. Always attend your court hearing regardless of the season.
What if my landlord won't make repairs?
Minnesota law requires landlords to maintain habitable conditions. Document everything with photos and written requests. You may have remedies including: rent escrow (paying rent to the court), repair-and-deduct, or using habitability as a defense. Upload all documentation to Semptify to build your case.
Can I sue my landlord back?
Yes! A counterclaim allows you to sue your landlord in the same case. Common counterclaims include: habitability violations, illegal lockouts, retaliation, security deposit violations, and discrimination. Use Semptify's Counterclaim Builder to identify what claims apply.
Do I need a lawyer?
You can represent yourself (pro se), and Semptify is designed to help you do so. However, a lawyer can significantly improve your chances. Contact Legal Aid (1-888-354-5522) to see if you qualify for free representation. HOME Line (1-800-745-6686) offers free counseling.
What should I bring to court?
Essential items: Your answer/response, all evidence documents (organized chronologically), rent receipts, lease, photos, any communication with landlord, timeline of events, and your ID. Semptify's Document Vault provides court-ready certificates for all your evidence.
