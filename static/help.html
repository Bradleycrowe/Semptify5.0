❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is Semptify? ▼
Semptify is a free housing rights platform that helps tenants, advocates, and housing professionals organize documents, track timelines, and understand their rights. We are not a law firm and do not provide legal advice — we provide education and organization tools.
Is Semptify free? ▼
Yes. Semptify is free forever for all users. There is no advertising, no premium tier, and no monetization of user data. Our mission is to help people who cannot afford a legal team protect their housing rights.
Where are my documents stored? ▼
Your documents are stored in your own cloud storage (Google Drive or Dropbox). Semptify never holds your full documents on our servers. We only store a metadata "overlay" — key dates, document type, parties — for search and timeline features. You own and control your files entirely.
What is the Vault Door? ▼
The Vault Door (🔒 button in the header) is the single entry point for uploading all documents to your case. Click it, select files, choose the document type, and fill in metadata. Your files go directly to your cloud storage with a blockchain timestamp for proof of authenticity.
Can Semptify give me legal advice? ▼
No. Semptify provides legal education, rights information, and organizational tools — not legal advice. For legal advice, contact a licensed attorney or free legal aid organization in your area. The Library and Help pages link to free resources.
I received an eviction notice. What do I do? ▼
Act immediately — deadlines are strict. First: upload the notice to your vault using the Vault Door. Second: use the Deadline Calculator to find your response deadline. Third: read the Eviction Process Guide in the Library. Fourth: contact legal aid or an advocate through the Help page. Do not ignore the notice.
How do I find an advocate or attorney? ▼
Use the Crisis Resources section to find free legal aid by location. You can also call 211, visit LawHelp.org, or contact your local legal aid society. Semptify does not provide referrals directly — we link to established organizations.
What is the Timeline / Journal? ▼
The Timeline is a chronological record of every event in your tenancy — communications, payments, repairs, notices, violations. It links to documents in your vault and can be exported as evidence for court or advocates. Keeping it updated is one of the most powerful things you can do to protect your rights.
