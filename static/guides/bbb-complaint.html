📋 Why File with BBB?

The Better Business Bureau creates public pressure on businesses. Unlike government agencies, BBB complaints become public record and directly affect how future customers view the business.

📊 Public Impact:

BBB profiles show complaint history, response rates, and customer reviews. Property management companies that rely on new tenant applications care deeply about their BBB rating.

📝 How to File a BBB Complaint

  1. Go to BBB.org: Visit bbb.org/file-a-complaint
  2. Search for the business: Enter landlord or property management company name
  3. Create an account: You'll need to register to file (it's free)
  4. Select complaint type: Choose the category that best fits your issue
  5. Describe your complaint: Be specific, factual, and professional
  6. State desired resolution: What outcome do you want?
  7. Attach supporting documents: Lease, correspondence, photos
  8. Submit and track: You'll receive updates as the business responds

✍️ Writing an Effective BBB Complaint

BBB complaints are public, so write them to influence future tenants:

📌 Key Elements:
  • Be factual: Stick to provable facts and dates
  • Be specific: Include dollar amounts, dates, and names
  • Be professional: Avoid emotional language or insults
  • Focus on patterns: If this is part of a pattern, say so
  • State your resolution: Be clear about what would resolve the issue
💡 Sample Opening:

"I am a tenant at [property name] managed by [company name]. On [date], I moved out after providing proper notice. Despite Minnesota law requiring return of my security deposit within 21 days (MN Stat. 504B.178), I have not received my deposit of $[amount] as of [date]. My attempts to contact [company] on [dates] have been ignored."

⏱️ What Happens After Filing

  1. BBB sends to business: They forward your complaint within 2 business days
  2. Business response window: Company has 14 days to respond
  3. You can reply: If unsatisfied, you can respond to their response
  4. Case closes: BBB marks it resolved, unresolved, or no response
  5. Becomes public: Your complaint appears on their BBB profile
⚠️ Note:

BBB cannot force a business to respond or take action. However, unanswered complaints significantly damage a business's BBB rating and public reputation.

📎 What to Include

  • Your lease agreement
  • Move-in/move-out inspection reports
  • Written communication with the landlord
  • Security deposit receipt
  • Any itemization you received
  • Photos of property condition
  • Timeline of events
  • Evidence of your attempts to resolve

🎯 When BBB Works Best

  • Property management companies that value reputation
  • Large landlords who rely on online presence
  • Businesses that are BBB accredited (they care most)
  • Clear-cut violations with good documentation
  • As part of a multi-agency strategy
💡 Pro Tip:

Check if the business is BBB accredited before filing. Accredited businesses pay fees and must maintain standards - they have more to lose from complaints.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is my complaint really public?
Yes! After the complaint process closes, a summary appears on the business's BBB profile. Your personal contact info is kept private, but the nature of your complaint is visible.
Can the business sue me for filing?
As long as your complaint is truthful, you're protected. Never lie or exaggerate, but don't be afraid to share factual experiences.
What if they don't respond?
Non-response is recorded and damages their rating. The complaint still becomes public, showing they ignored you.
Can I file anonymously?
BBB requires your contact information, but only shares your name with the business - not publicly. However, the business will know you filed.
Should I file with BBB and government agencies?
Yes! BBB creates public pressure while government agencies have enforcement powers. Use both as part of a multi-agency strategy.
