📋 What is Legal Aid?

Legal Aid provides free legal services to low-income Minnesotans. If you qualify, they can represent you in court, help with legal documents, and provide strategic advice for your case.

✅ Why Get Legal Aid?

Having a lawyer can dramatically improve your chances of success. Legal Aid attorneys understand landlord-tenant law and can negotiate from a position of strength.

💰 Income Guidelines (2024)

Legal Aid generally serves households at or below 200% of federal poverty guidelines:

📞 How to Apply for Legal Aid

  1. Call the intake line: 1-888-354-5522 (Mon-Fri, 8am-4:30pm)
  2. Complete the screening: Answer questions about your income, household size, and legal issue
  3. Provide documentation: Be ready to share proof of income, your lease, and any court papers
  4. Wait for assignment: If you qualify, your case will be assigned to an attorney
  5. Work with your attorney: Once assigned, they'll contact you to discuss your case
⚠️ Apply Early!

Legal Aid has limited resources. If you're facing eviction, apply as soon as possible - don't wait until your court date!

📝 What to Have Ready

  • Proof of income (pay stubs, benefits letter, tax returns)
  • Your lease agreement
  • Any court papers you've received
  • Written notices from your landlord
  • Documentation of the problem (photos, repair requests)
  • Your landlord's contact information
  • List of important dates (when problems started, deadlines)

✅ What Legal Aid Can Help With

  • Eviction defense and representation in court
  • Negotiating with landlords
  • Security deposit recovery
  • Habitability and repair issues
  • Discrimination complaints
  • Lease violations and terminations
  • Lockouts and illegal evictions

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don't qualify for Legal Aid?
You can still get help from HOME Line (free tenant advice), represent yourself with Semptify's help, or contact the Volunteer Lawyers Network for limited assistance.
How long does it take to get an attorney?
It depends on case urgency and available resources. Emergency eviction cases are prioritized. You may get a callback within a few days.
Can Legal Aid help with complaints to agencies?
Yes! They can help you file complaints with the Attorney General, HUD, and other agencies, and advise on the best strategy for your situation.
What if I have an emergency?
Call immediately and explain the urgency. If you're being locked out or facing imminent eviction, mention this right away.
