Multi-Agency Strategy

Maximize pressure by filing with multiple agencies strategically

💪 Why File Multiple Complaints?

When bad landlords face complaints from multiple agencies simultaneously, they're more likely to settle and change their behavior.

📋 The Power of Multiple Fronts

Each agency has different powers, focus areas, and timelines. By filing strategically with multiple agencies, you:

  • Create pressure from multiple directions simultaneously
  • Increase the chance that at least one agency takes action
  • Build a documented record across multiple systems
  • Make it harder for landlords to ignore your complaints
  • Potentially trigger investigations that help other tenants too
✅ Real Impact:

Landlords who receive complaints from multiple agencies often settle faster because the cost of fighting on multiple fronts is too high.

✅ Best Practices

  • Keep consistent facts: All complaints should tell the same story
  • Cross-reference: Mention other complaints in each filing
  • Document everything: Save all confirmation numbers and correspondence
  • Respond promptly: When agencies contact you, respond immediately
  • Stay professional: Stick to facts, avoid emotional language
  • Update all agencies: If anything changes, notify everyone
⚠️ Important:

Never lie or exaggerate in any complaint. Each agency may share information, and inconsistencies will hurt your credibility.
