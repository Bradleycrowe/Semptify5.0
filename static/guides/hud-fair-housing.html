📋 What is the Fair Housing Act?

The Fair Housing Act prohibits discrimination in housing based on protected classes. If your landlord treated you differently because of who you are, you may have a fair housing complaint.

⚡ HUD Has Federal Power!

HUD can investigate, issue charges, and refer cases to the Department of Justice for prosecution. Landlords can face significant penalties for fair housing violations.

🏛️ Minnesota Additional Protections:

Minnesota also protects: marital status, public assistance status, sexual orientation, gender identity, and age (for seniors).

🚨 Examples of Housing Discrimination

  • Refusing to rent because of your race, religion, or national origin
  • Charging higher rent or deposits to certain groups
  • Different lease terms based on family status
  • Refusing reasonable accommodations for disabilities
  • Sexual harassment by landlord or property staff
  • "No children" policies (unless senior housing)
  • Steering tenants to certain units based on race
  • Different maintenance response times based on protected class
  • Retaliating because you complained about discrimination

📝 How to File a HUD Complaint

  1. Go to the HUD portal: Visit HUD's online complaint form
  2. Provide your information: Name, address, phone, and email
  3. Identify the respondent: Landlord name, property address, and contact info
  4. Select the protected class: Which protected characteristic was the basis for discrimination?
  5. Describe what happened: Be specific - dates, what was said, who witnessed it
  6. Explain how it's discriminatory: Why do you believe this was based on your protected class?
  7. Upload evidence: Emails, texts, witness statements, recordings (if legal)
  8. Submit and save: Keep your complaint number

📎 Evidence to Gather

  • Written communications showing discriminatory statements
  • Rental application and any rejection letters
  • Your lease and any addendums
  • Names and contact info of witnesses
  • Records of different treatment (if others were treated better)
  • Photos or videos if relevant
  • Timeline of events
  • Proof of your protected class status (if needed)
💡 Comparators Help:

If you can show that someone outside your protected class was treated better in the same situation, that strengthens your case significantly.

⏱️ What Happens After Filing

  1. Intake review: HUD reviews your complaint for jurisdiction (10 days)
  2. Notification: HUD notifies the landlord and offers conciliation
  3. Investigation: HUD investigates (100 days target)
  4. Determination: HUD decides if there's reasonable cause
  5. Resolution: Settlement, administrative hearing, or referral to DOJ
💰 Potential Remedies:
  • Monetary damages (actual damages + emotional distress)
  • Civil penalties up to $150,000+ for repeat offenders
  • Injunctive relief (landlord must change practices)
  • Attorney's fees if you hire a lawyer

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file if I'm undocumented?
Yes! Fair housing protections apply to everyone regardless of immigration status. HUD does not ask about or report immigration status.
What if I can't prove discrimination?
Direct evidence isn't always required. Circumstantial evidence (patterns, comparators, timing) can be enough. Let HUD investigate.
Can my landlord retaliate?
Retaliation is illegal and is itself a fair housing violation. If your landlord retaliates, report it immediately to HUD.
Should I also file with MN Department of Human Rights?
You can file with both! Minnesota's Department of Human Rights handles state-level discrimination complaints and may have additional protections.
