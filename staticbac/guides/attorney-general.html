📋 Why File with the Attorney General?

The Minnesota Attorney General's Office has strong enforcement powers to investigate and prosecute businesses that engage in fraud, deceptive practices, or consumer protection violations.

⚡ AG Has Teeth!

Unlike some agencies, the AG can file lawsuits, seek injunctions, and impose significant penalties. Landlords take AG complaints seriously.

🚨 When to File with the AG

The Attorney General handles complaints involving:

🎭 Deceptive Practices

False advertising, misleading lease terms, hidden fees, bait-and-switch tactics

💰 Security Deposit Fraud

Wrongful withholding, fake deductions, failure to return within 21 days

📄 Lease Violations

Illegal lease terms, unfair contract provisions, unconscionable clauses

🔒 Illegal Lockouts

Self-help evictions, utility shutoffs, harassment to force move-out

🏚️ Habitability Issues

Refusing to make repairs, dangerous conditions, code violations

💵 Fee Abuses

Excessive late fees, illegal charges, improper rent increases

📝 How to File a Complaint

  1. Go to the AG website: Visit ag.state.mn.us/Office/Complaint.asp
  2. Select complaint type: Choose "Consumer Protection" or the most relevant category
  3. Provide your information: Name, address, phone, email
  4. Identify the business: Landlord/property management company name and full address
  5. Describe the problem: Be specific about what happened, when, and what laws were violated
  6. Attach documentation: Upload your lease, notices, photos, and any evidence
  7. State your desired resolution: What do you want to happen?
  8. Submit and save confirmation: Keep your complaint number for reference

📎 Documents to Include

  • Your lease agreement (full copy)
  • All written communication with landlord
  • Rent payment records
  • Security deposit receipt and any itemization
  • Photos of property conditions
  • Repair requests you've made
  • Any notices you've received
  • Timeline of events

✍️ Writing an Effective Complaint

📌 Key Elements of a Strong Complaint:
  • Be specific: Include dates, amounts, and names
  • Cite violations: Reference specific laws (e.g., MN Stat. 504B.178 for security deposits)
  • Show pattern: If others are affected, mention that
  • Document harm: Explain how you've been damaged financially or otherwise
  • Request action: Be clear about what resolution you seek
💡 Sample Language:

"On [date], [landlord name] failed to return my security deposit of $[amount] within the required 21 days as mandated by MN Stat. 504B.178. Despite written requests on [dates], I have received no response. This constitutes a violation of Minnesota tenant protection laws and deceptive business practices."

⏱️ What Happens After Filing

  1. Confirmation: You'll receive a complaint number and confirmation
  2. Review: AG staff reviews your complaint (may take several weeks)
  3. Contact to business: AG may send a letter to the landlord requesting response
  4. Investigation: If warranted, further investigation may occur
  5. Resolution: AG may mediate, take enforcement action, or refer to other agencies
⏰ Timeline: Initial review typically takes 2-4 weeks. Complex cases may take several months. You'll receive updates on your complaint status.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Will the AG represent me in court?
No, the AG doesn't represent individual consumers. However, they can take action against businesses on behalf of all Minnesota consumers, which may benefit your case.
Can I still sue my landlord after filing?
Yes! Filing with the AG doesn't prevent you from pursuing your own legal action. In fact, AG involvement can strengthen your case.
Is there a fee to file?
No, filing a complaint with the AG is completely free.
Will my landlord know I filed?
Yes, if the AG contacts your landlord, they will know you filed. However, retaliation is illegal under Minnesota law.
