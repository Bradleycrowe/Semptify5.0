👤 Who Are You?

Semptify is built for everyone in the housing ecosystem — tenants, advocates, and legal professionals.
Choose the role that matches how you'll use the platform.

📓 Remember: Semptify documents facts from both perspectives. Whether you're a tenant organizing your records or an advocate helping clients, the goal is always clear, honest documentation — not taking sides.

🏠
Tenant
I rent my home
A renter who wants to understand both your rights AND responsibilities, keep good records, and be prepared — just in case.
Best For
Renters who want to document their tenancy properly, understand what they owe and what they're owed, and avoid problems through good records
  • 📓 Personal tenant journal
  • 📋 Document timeline builder
  • 📚 Rights AND responsibilities guides
  • ☁️ Private cloud storage (your Drive/Dropbox)
  • ⚖️ Evidence organization (if ever needed)
✓ No verification needed
🤝
Housing Advocate
I help tenants professionally
A housing professional helping tenants stay organized, understand their situation, and document facts accurately.
Best For
HOME Line counselors, Legal Aid staff, HUD-certified counselors, social workers helping tenants document and understand their housing situation
  • 📓 Everything in Tenant, plus:
  • 👥 Client case management
  • 📊 Multi-client document oversight
  • 🔄 Document delivery to clients
  • 📋 Referral coordination tools
🎟️ Invite code required
⚖️
Licensed Attorney
I'm a lawyer in Minnesota
A licensed attorney using Semptify to help clients organize evidence and communicate securely — while maintaining professional standards.
Best For
Housing attorneys, pro bono lawyers, Legal Aid attorneys who need secure document collaboration with clients
  • ⚖️ Everything in Advocate, plus:
  • 🔒 Privileged communication channels
  • 📝 Work product protection markers
  • ⚠️ Conflict checking system
  • 📤 Secure document delivery to clients
🔐 Bar verification required
⚠️ Legal Role Notice: This role is for licensed attorneys only. Attorney-client privilege requires proper licensure. Verification protects both you and your clients.

Not sure which to choose? Most people should select "Tenant" — even if you later work with an advocate or attorney.

Your role determines what features you see, but you can always change it later.

💡 Tip: If you're a tenant working with a lawyer, stay as "Tenant" — your attorney can use their Legal role to communicate with you.
